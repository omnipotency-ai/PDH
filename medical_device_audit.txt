⏺ Work Queue Strategic Audit

  The Big Picture

  You have 102 open items across 6 sprints. But the market fit audit changes
  everything about how to prioritize them. Roughly half the queue is fixing
  legitimate engineering problems. The other half is polishing or extending an
  architecture that's built around the wrong premise — that this is a clinical
  recovery tool for post-surgical patients.

  Before I break down themes, here's the uncomfortable truth: the app's entire 
  identity is medical device territory, not just a few red flags. The "Dr. Poo"
  persona is introduced as a "clinical nutritionist" and "clinical reasoning
  engine." Your landing page says "AI gastroenterologist." You collect surgery
  types, post-op dates, comorbidities, and medications. You calculate transit
  windows based on surgical procedure type. The food verdict system uses
  "safe/avoid/watch/culprit" — which maps to clinical trial language.

  This isn't a few labels to rename. It's the app's core narrative.

  Theme 2: Leaky Pipes You're Patching (STOP — rethink the plumbing)

  These are items where you're fixing or extending systems that are
  architecturally built for the wrong product. Patching them deepens your
  medical device exposure.

  2A. The Clinical AI Identity (MUST rethink)

  The entire Dr. Poo prompt system (aiAnalysis.ts — 1953 LOC, the biggest file
  in the app) is built as a clinical reasoning engine. Key problems:

  - System prompt (line 1314): "You are Dr. Poo, a clinical nutritionist
  specialising in post-operative colon reconnection recovery"
  - Line 1351: "You are a clinical reasoning engine... your medical knowledge of
   post-anastomosis physiology"
  - Bristol interpretation (line 1501): "Bristol 1: Hard lumps — RISKY.
  Constipation, dangerous straining on the anastomosis site"
  - Stethoscope icon in the UI
  - "AI gastroenterologist" on the landing page
  - Tone options include "analytical/clinical" with "correct medical
  terminology"

  WQ-108 (split aiAnalysis.ts) and WQ-318 (move context compiler server-side)
  are patching this pipe. You'd be refactoring and optimizing a prompt
  architecture that needs to be fundamentally rewritten with wellness framing
  first.

  What to do instead: Rewrite the Dr. Poo persona as a wellness coach/food
  journal companion before touching any of the optimization work. The split and
  server migration should happen after the persona is correct.

  2B. The Surgery-Specific Data Model

  Your schema collects:
  - Surgery type enum (ileostomy reversal, colostomy reversal, etc.) —
  convex/validators.ts:541-548
  - Surgery date — used to calculate getDaysPostOp() — aiAnalysis.ts:300-306
  - Recovery stage (zones 1/2/3) — convex/schema.ts:89
  - Comorbidities, medications, allergies — validators.ts:555-596
  - Reproductive health (feature-flagged but in schema)

  WQ-173 (health profile scope creep — 45+ fields) is literally flagging this
  problem but frames it as "trim non-essential fields." It's not a trimming
  exercise — the surgical data model IS the medical device classification 
  trigger.

  What to do instead: Redesign the health profile as a wellness profile. Surgery
   history becomes an optional "dietary context" field (free text, not an enum
  of surgical procedures). Recovery stages become "dietary phases"
  (introduction/expansion/maintenance). Comorbidities become "dietary
  considerations."

  2C. The Food Evidence Pipeline (safe/avoid/watch/culprit)

  The food classification system uses clinical trial language:
  - "safe", "avoid", "watch", "culprit" — convex/schema.ts:192-227
  - Zone system (1/2/3) explicitly tied to surgical recovery —
  foodStatusThresholds.ts:52-71
  - Transit windows calculated by surgery type — aiAnalysis.ts:318-340
  - Bristol interpretation with "RISKY"/"dehydration risk" language —
  foodStatusThresholds.ts:156

  Several open items polish this system (WQ-114 next food logic, WQ-126 next
  food to try, WQ-129 safe foods confidence labels, WQ-135 trial history). You'd
   be extending a classification system that sounds like a clinical drug trial.

  What to do instead: Reframe the entire vocabulary. "Safe" → "agrees with me."
  "Avoid" → "doesn't agree with me." "Watch" → "still figuring out." "Culprit" →
   "might be a trigger." "Trial" → "experience." "Zone" → "food group" or
  "category." This isn't just label changes — it changes how the AI reasons
  about the data too.

  2D. The Transit Map

  Sprint 2.7 (10 open items, WQ-320–329) builds a radial SVG transit map. The
  transit map is paused and you're working on geometry — but the underlying
  metaphor is digestive transit through the GI tract. The zone rings literally
  represent parts of the colon.

  Assessment: The transit map metaphor is actually your biggest opportunity. A
  food journey map is a wellness concept. But the current implementation labels
  zones after GI anatomy. If you reframe zones as food experience stages (not
  anatomical stages), the map works for wellness.
