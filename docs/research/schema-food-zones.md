**Zone 1 should only contain very low‑residue, low‑fat, low‑irritant foods in liquid or soft/purée form, with minimal insoluble fiber and no “bitty” textures.**  
**Below is a structured list split into Carbs, Proteins, and Fats/Dairy (by macro role), including specific gentle preparations plus metadata fields: osmotic, residue, fiber type, gas‑producing, and dryness.** [elht.nhs](https://elht.nhs.uk/application/files/3315/8556/0327/DIET-002-LowFibrelowresiduediet-2019.pdf)

***

## Metadata fields (schema)

For every Zone‑1 food, you can attach:

- **osmotic_effect**: none / low / moderate / high (sugar/alcohol load). [embracingostomylife](https://embracingostomylife.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/12/UOAA-Post-op-nutritional-guidelines.pdf)
- **total_residue**: very_low / low / moderate (skins, husks, gristle). [webmd](https://www.webmd.com/ibd-crohns-disease/crohns-disease/low-residue-diet-foods)
- **fiber_total**: g per typical serving; **fiber_insoluble**: low / moderate; **fiber_soluble**: low / moderate. [compgihealth](https://compgihealth.com/2025/04/low-residue-diet/)
- **gas_producing**: yes / no / possible (beans, brassicas, carbonation excluded from Zone 1). [uofmhealth](https://www.uofmhealth.org/sites/default/files/2024-11/LowFiberLowResidueDiet.pdf)
- **dry_texture**: yes / no (Zone 1 is almost all “no”; dry foods move to Zone 2+). [health.ucdavis](https://health.ucdavis.edu/surgery/documents/ileostomy-nutrition.pdf)
- **irritant_load**: none / low (no chili, pepper, garlic, onion). [webmd](https://www.webmd.com/ibd-crohns-disease/crohns-disease/low-residue-diet-foods)

You can keep everything here as: **very_low residue, low fat, no skin/seed, no spice**, unless noted.

## Carbs – Zone 1

These are refined, low‑fiber carbohydrates in liquid, purée, or very soft form. [elht.nhs](https://elht.nhs.uk/application/files/3315/8556/0327/DIET-002-LowFibrelowresiduediet-2019.pdf)

| Food (Zone 1 form)                          | Notes and preparation detail                                | Key metadata (typical) |
|--------------------------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------|------------------------|
| Clear vegetable broth (strained, no bits)  | Commercial or homemade; fully strained.  [embracingostomylife](https://embracingostomylife.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/12/UOAA-Post-op-nutritional-guidelines.pdf)   | osmotic: low; residue: very_low; fiber_total: ~0; insoluble: low; soluble: low; gas: no; dry: no; irritant: none if unseasoned. |
| Clear chicken broth (strained)             | No skin/shreds; just flavored fluid.  [embracingostomylife](https://embracingostomylife.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/12/UOAA-Post-op-nutritional-guidelines.pdf)      | As above. |
| Clear beef broth (strained)                | Lean stock, skimmed fat.  [embracingostomylife](https://embracingostomylife.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/12/UOAA-Post-op-nutritional-guidelines.pdf)                          | As above; maybe slightly higher fat but still “low”. |
| Consommé (clear clarified soup)           | No vegetable pieces, just clarified stock.                 | Same as clear broth. |
| Miso soup (well strained, no seaweed/tofu bits) | Mild miso only; no wakame, tofu cubes, spring onion.  [compgihealth](https://compgihealth.com/2025/04/low-residue-diet/) | osmotic: low–moderate (salt); residue: very_low; fiber: ~0; gas: possible (fermented, but usually fine in small amount). |
| Clear strained tomato broth (no pulp/seeds) | Very diluted, non‑acidic; no skins/seeds.  [elht.nhs](https://elht.nhs.uk/application/files/3315/8556/0327/DIET-002-LowFibrelowresiduediet-2019.pdf) | osmotic: low; residue: low; fiber: very_low; irritant: low if not acidic/spiced. |
| Plain gelatin / jelly (no fruit pieces)    | Classic “Jell‑O” style dessert, not sugar‑free at first if sorbitol.  [embracingostomylife](https://embracingostomylife.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/12/UOAA-Post-op-nutritional-guidelines.pdf) | osmotic: moderate (sugar); residue: very_low; fiber: 0; gas: no; dry: no. |
| Plain salted crackers in broth (soaked until soft) | White, seed‑free crackers softened in soup.  [webmd](https://www.webmd.com/ibd-crohns-disease/crohns-disease/low-residue-diet-foods) | residue: low; fiber: very_low; dry: no (because soaked); gas: no. |

| Plain mashed potato (no skin, smooth, low‑fat) | Well‑cooked, sieved/mashed with a little lactose‑free milk or water.  [elht.nhs](https://elht.nhs.uk/application/files/3315/8556/0327/DIET-002-LowFibrelowresiduediet-2019.pdf) | fiber_total: low; insoluble: low; soluble: low–moderate; gas: no; dry: no. |
| Plain mashed sweet potato (peeled, smooth, low‑fat) | Only if tolerated; more soluble fiber than white potato.  [compgihealth](https://compgihealth.com/2025/04/low-residue-diet/) | residue: low; fiber_total: low–moderate; gas: possible. |
| Plain white rice (overcooked, slightly “soupy”) | Cooked soft, maybe in extra water or broth.  [elht.nhs](https://elht.nhs.uk/application/files/3315/8556/0327/DIET-002-LowFibrelowresiduediet-2019.pdf) | residue: very_low; fiber: ~0–low; osmotic: low. |
| Plain white pasta (overcooked, soft, no sauce) | Tiny shapes, cooked beyond al dente.  [elht.nhs](https://elht.nhs.uk/application/files/3315/8556/0327/DIET-002-LowFibrelowresiduediet-2019.pdf)       | residue: low; fiber: low; dry: no (served moist). |
| Cream of wheat / farina / semolina porridge | Made with water or lactose‑free milk; smooth.  [webmd](https://www.webmd.com/ibd-crohns-disease/crohns-disease/low-residue-diet-foods) | fiber_total: low; residue: low; gas: no. |
| Low‑fiber instant oats (finely milled, well cooked) | Only plain, no nuts/dried fruit.  [webmd](https://www.webmd.com/ibd-crohns-disease/crohns-disease/low-residue-diet-foods)                  | fiber_total: low; more soluble; gas: possible if large serving. |
| Tapioca / sago / rice pudding (no skins)   | Soft, milky puddings, low residue.  [uhsussex.nhs](https://www.uhsussex.nhs.uk/wp-content/uploads/2022/04/2109.1-Low-residue-diet-2024.pdf)                | osmotic: moderate (sugar); residue: very_low; fiber: 0. |
| Plain couscous / polenta (soft, fine)      | No added veg/herbs; very soft.  [uhsussex.nhs](https://www.uhsussex.nhs.uk/wp-content/uploads/2022/04/2109.1-Low-residue-diet-2024.pdf)                    | residue: low; fiber: low; gas: no. |

## Proteins – Zone 1

Aim for very lean, moist, non‑fibrous proteins: poached, boiled, steamed, or finely minced in broth. [compgihealth](https://compgihealth.com/2025/04/low-residue-diet/)

| Food (Zone 1 form)                            | Notes and preparation detail                                         | Key metadata (typical) |
|----------------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------|------------------------|
| Poached chicken breast                        | Skinless, no browning, cooked in water/broth, shredded finely if needed.  [elht.nhs](https://elht.nhs.uk/application/files/3315/8556/0327/DIET-002-LowFibrelowresiduediet-2019.pdf) | residue: low; fiber: 0; gas: no; dry: low if served with broth. |
| Boiled chicken breast                         | Same as above; can be diced small for soup.                          | As above. |
| Poached white fish (cod, hake, sole)          | Cooked in water/stock; no batter, no frying, no crust.  [compgihealth](https://compgihealth.com/2025/04/low-residue-diet/)      | residue: low; fiber: 0; gas: no. |
| Steamed white fish (very plain)               | Lightly steamed, moist, no char.                                     | As above. |
| Poached egg                                   | Soft, runny or just‑set yolk; no fried edges.  [compgihealth](https://compgihealth.com/2025/04/low-residue-diet/)               | residue: very_low; fiber: 0; fat: moderate; osmotic: none. |
| Soft‑boiled egg                               | Same profile as poached; peel carefully.                             | As above. |
| Scrambled egg (very soft, low‑fat)            | Made with a splash of lactose‑free milk/water, not browned.          | residue: very_low; fat: low–moderate; gas: no. |
| Lean turkey breast (boiled/poached)           | Same rules as chicken.                                               | residue: low; fiber: 0. |
| Very soft tofu (silken)                       | Plain, in broth or miso‑style soup, no seaweed/onion.  [compgihealth](https://compgihealth.com/2025/04/low-residue-diet/)       | fiber: ~0; residue: very_low; gas: possible in some, so flag. |
| Protein‑fortified clear drink (medical shake, clear whey) | e.g. clear whey or hospital‑style protein water.  [embracingostomylife](https://embracingostomylife.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/12/UOAA-Post-op-nutritional-guidelines.pdf) | osmotic: moderate (if sugary); residue: 0; fiber: 0; gas: possible. |
| Plain gelatin with added protein (if available) | Some medical gels; no fruit.                                         | As above. |
| Strained chicken purée soup                   | Chicken blended into smooth soup, then strained.                     | residue: low; fiber: 0. |
| Strained lentil/broth‑based protein soup (heavily diluted, no skins) | Very cautious/late Zone 1 if you want a plant‑protein test; otherwise push to Zone 2.  [compgihealth](https://compgihealth.com/2025/04/low-residue-diet/) | residue: low–moderate; fiber_soluble: some; gas: possible → maybe better to keep out of Zone 1. |

For strict Zone 1, you may decide to **exclude legumes entirely** and keep protein to **egg, poultry, white fish, tofu** in ultra‑gentle forms. [uofmhealth](https://www.uofmhealth.org/sites/default/files/2024-11/LowFiberLowResidueDiet.pdf)

## Fats & Dairy (by macro role) – Zone 1

Many guidelines allow **small amounts of low‑fat dairy and simple added fats** even in early low‑residue phases, mainly to improve energy intake. [health.ucdavis](https://health.ucdavis.edu/surgery/documents/ileostomy-nutrition.pdf)

### Dairy as “protein‑lean”

| Food (Zone 1 form)                      | Notes and preparation                               | Key metadata |
|----------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------|--------------|
| Lactose‑free milk (diluted if needed)  | Small servings, used in porridge/purée.  [webmd](https://www.webmd.com/ibd-crohns-disease/crohns-disease/low-residue-diet-foods) | osmotic: moderate (lactose‑free still sugary); residue: 0; fiber: 0; gas: possible in some. |
| Lactose‑free yogurt (plain, low‑fat, smooth) | No seeds/fruit; can be mixed into mashed potato or porridge.  [webmd](https://www.webmd.com/ibd-crohns-disease/crohns-disease/low-residue-diet-foods) | residue: very_low; fiber: 0; osmotic: moderate; gas: possible. |
| Plain low‑fat Greek‑style yogurt (strained) | Higher protein, lower lactose; no toppings.        | residue: very_low; fiber: 0. |
| Mild soft cheese (e.g. queso fresco, ricotta, cottage cheese but smooth/strained) | No fruit/seed; only in small amounts.  [compgihealth](https://compgihealth.com/2025/04/low-residue-diet/) | fat: low–moderate; residue: very_low. |
| Custard / milk pudding (smooth, no skin, no fruit) | Classic hospital dessert, low‑residue.  [elht.nhs](https://elht.nhs.uk/application/files/3315/8556/0327/DIET-002-LowFibrelowresiduediet-2019.pdf) | osmotic: moderate (sugar); residue: very_low; fiber: 0. |

### Fats as “added fat”

| Food (Zone 1 form)                 | Notes                                                  | Key metadata |
|-----------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------|--------------|
| Small amount of butter in mash or purée | 1–2 teaspoons per serving to aid palatability.  [webmd](https://www.webmd.com/ibd-crohns-disease/crohns-disease/low-residue-diet-foods) | fat: low per serving; residue: 0; osmotic: none. |
| Small amount of refined vegetable oil | e.g. sunflower/olive oil added to purées.             | As above. |
| Cream added to soup (tiny quantity)    | For energy density; watch for lactose.                | fat: moderate; osmotic: low–moderate if lactose‑rich. |

Zone‑1 rule: **fat present only in support of texture and calories**, not as a stand‑alone high‑fat food (no cream‑heavy sauces, fried foods, or cheese plates). [webmd](https://www.webmd.com/ibd-crohns-disease/crohns-disease/low-residue-diet-foods)

## Vegetables & Fruits – Zone 1 (strictly purée/strained)

Most low‑residue protocols allow **limited, very well‑cooked, peeled, seedless veg** even early, often starting with carrots/pumpkin. [ruh.nhs](https://www.ruh.nhs.uk/patients/patient_information/GAS169_Low_residue_diet.pdf)

### Vegetable carbs

| Food (Zone 1 form)                          | Notes and preparation                                   | Key metadata |
|--------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------|--------------|
| Carrot purée (well‑cooked, peeled, smooth) | No skins, no chunks; blended with water/broth.  [elht.nhs](https://elht.nhs.uk/application/files/3315/8556/0327/DIET-002-LowFibrelowresiduediet-2019.pdf) | fiber_total: low–moderate; insoluble: low (if well sieved); soluble: moderate; residue: low; gas: no. |
| Pumpkin / butternut squash purée           | Peeled, seeded, well‑cooked, blended smooth.  [compgihealth](https://compgihealth.com/2025/04/low-residue-diet/)   | similar to carrot; slightly more soluble fiber. |
| Potato–carrot mixed purée                  | Same constraints; more bland baseline.                  | As above. |
| Strained vegetable soup (carrot, potato)   | Cooked veg fully blended and sieved.  [elht.nhs](https://elht.nhs.uk/application/files/3315/8556/0327/DIET-002-LowFibrelowresiduediet-2019.pdf)   | residue: very_low; fiber: low; gas: no. |

### Fruit in Zone 1 (if you allow any)

Most guidelines treat fruits as **careful/limited** even on low‑residue. For strict baseline you might only allow: [compgihealth](https://compgihealth.com/2025/04/low-residue-diet/)

| Food (Zone 1 form)                   | Notes                                      | Key metadata |
|-------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------|--------------|
| Applesauce (smooth, no peel, no chunks) | Unsweetened or lightly sweetened.  [webmd](https://www.webmd.com/ibd-crohns-disease/crohns-disease/low-residue-diet-foods) | fiber_total: low–moderate, mainly soluble; residue: low; osmotic: moderate (fructose); gas: possible. |
| Very ripe banana (mashed, small portion) | If used, I’d treat as edge of Zone 1 / early Zone 2.  [webmd](https://www.webmd.com/ibd-crohns-disease/crohns-disease/low-residue-diet-foods) | soluble fiber: some; osmotic: moderate; gas: possible. |

If you want **ultra‑conservative Zone 1**, you could push all fruit to Zone 2 and keep Zone 1 fruit‑free. [uofmhealth](https://www.uofmhealth.org/sites/default/files/2024-11/LowFiberLowResidueDiet.pdf)

## Dry‑texture check (Caca Traca specific)

Zone 1 should **exclude** foods that are intrinsically dry and require a lot of chewing, even if low‑residue on paper (e.g. plain toast, crackers by themselves). [embracingostomylife](https://embracingostomylife.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/12/UOAA-Post-op-nutritional-guidelines.pdf)
Where you *do* use them (e.g. crackers, bread), they should be **soaked** in broth/milk until soft, so `dry_texture: no`. [uhb.nhs](https://www.uhb.nhs.uk/media/mzemzk2v/pi_dietetics_nutrition-a-guide-to-eating-if-you-have-an-iieostomy.pdf)
