# Snacks Metadata — Research Reference

> **Status (2026-03-15): Research reference, not implementation spec.** This content was used to inform the food registry during Phases 1–2.5. The actual registry is in `shared/foodRegistry.ts`. Snack categories are covered by `"plain cracker"` (Zone 1B), `"rice cracker"` (Zone 1B), `"concentrated sweet food"` (Zone 3), and `"deep fried food"` (Zone 3) in the current registry. This doc is kept for reference when expanding the registry in Phase 5.

---

**Yes, tucks, rice crackers, corn crackers, cream crackers, Ritz crackers, crispbread, breadsticks, and bayonets (likely "bayonetas," thin Spanish breadsticks) belong in the same "Crispy Crackers" or "Dry Savory Crackers" category as Quelitas in your Caca Traca app.**
**They share low-to-moderate fiber, dry/crunchy texture, and savory profiles, allowing consistent Zone 2 placement with metadata tweaks for fat, residue, or flavor risks.** [perplexity](https://www.perplexity.ai/search/b5a30651-a630-44f8-9224-1ad28b1feb14)

## Category Fit and UI Tips

Group under **"Crispy Crackers"** on Carbs/Texture lines in your transit map—progress from plain rice (Zone 1 unlock) to flavored Ritz (Zone 2). [perplexity](https://www.perplexity.ai/search/b5a30651-a630-44f8-9224-1ad28b1feb14)
Metadata: All dry texture (chew emphasis), low insoluble fiber (<5g/serving safe), fat 5-20g/100g, mild/no spice. Test one type at a time per your rules. [perplexity](https://www.perplexity.ai/search/2985de1c-5d54-40df-8800-20843bd637ad)
Exceptions: Spicy/wholegrain variants shift to Zone 3 (high residue/irritants). [perplexity](https://www.perplexity.ai/search/b5a30651-a630-44f8-9224-1ad28b1feb14)

**You can absolutely build out both Savoury Snacks and Sweetened Snacks with 15–20 varied, post-anastomosis‑relevant examples each.**  
**Below I’ve split them into Zone 2 vs Zone 3 candidates and kept forms varied (puffs, crisps, nuts, gummies, dried fruit, etc.).** [teamnutrition](https://teamnutrition.ca/blog-nutritionist-dietitian/top-10-salty-snacks)

Think “salty, snacky, often higher fat, usually low fiber.” Zone 2 = safer/plain; Zone 3 = spicier, fattier, or more fibrous.

### How to tag Savoury Snacks

- **Common properties:** salty, moderate–high fat, generally low fiber except legumes/leafy chips. [chefworks](https://www.chefworks.com/blog/10-healthy-snacks-that-will-satisfy-your-craving-for-something-crispy-and-crunchy)
- **Zone logic:**
  - Zone 2: plain crisps, mild crackers, pretzels, soft pretzels.
  - Zone 3: nuts, popcorn, olives, heavily flavored crisps, roasted legumes, leafy chips, fatty meat snacks. [perplexity](https://www.perplexity.ai/search/b5a30651-a630-44f8-9224-1ad28b1feb14)
- **UI hint:** Savoury Snacks line could branch from Crispy Crackers; Zone‑2 stations first (salted crisps/pretzels), then Zone‑3 “spicy/fibrous/fatty” stations unlocked later. [perplexity](https://www.perplexity.ai/search/b5a30651-a630-44f8-9224-1ad28b1feb14)

### How to tag Sweetened Snacks

- **Common properties:** high sugar, variable fiber (none in gummies; lots in dried fruit), often low protein. [theleangreenbean](https://www.theleangreenbean.com/healthy-fruit-snacks/)
- **Zone logic:**
  - Zone 2: low/no‑fiber sweets (gummies, marshmallows, hard candies, simple chocolate, fruit leather, sorbet) in small controlled amounts.
  - Zone 3: high‑fiber or combo sweets (dried fruit, prunes, trail mix, yogurt‑covered, chocolate‑covered dried fruit, higher‑cocoa bars). [perplexity](https://www.perplexity.ai/search/2985de1c-5d54-40df-8800-20843bd637ad)
- **UI hint:** Sweetened Snacks line could branch off the Carbs/Sugar theme: early stations = soft candies/jellies, later = dried fruits and mixed snacks. [perplexity](https://www.perplexity.ai/search/b5a30651-a630-44f8-9224-1ad28b1feb14)
